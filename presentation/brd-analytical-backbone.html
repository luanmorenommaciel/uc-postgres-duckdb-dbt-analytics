Analytical Backbone Initiative

Separating analytics from the storefront —
so insight stops competing with the order.

The business case for a dedicated analytical platform: the problem, its cost, and the verifiable outcomes this initiative must deliver. The "what," not the "how."

Prepared for the Executive Steering Committee
FY2026 · Data & Platform · Classification: Internal / Confidential
SECTION 01 · FOR THE COMMITTEE
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY · SITUATION · PROBLEM · ASK

One database runs the store and the reports. Both are losing.

We are a ~$500M GMV operator processing 50,000 orders a day, 75,000 at peak. Today a single operational database serves the storefront and every analytical query at once. The two workloads contend: heavy analytics lock resources during peak and slow order processing, while relentless write traffic makes reporting crawl. The cost of leaving this unsolved is ~$2.08M a year — and a data team spending most of its capacity firefighting, not building.

SITUATION

$500M GMV, one database

50K orders/day (75K peak), AOV $85, 28% gross margin, served by a data team of 8 — all on one shared system.

PROBLEM

Workloads contend

Analytics and the storefront fight for the same resources. Reports take minutes; peak order processing slows under analytical load.

THE ASK

Separate the two

Give analytics its own home so it stops contending with the store — faster insight, peak isolation, less operational toil.

THE DECISION REQUESTED
Approve an initiative to isolate the analytical workload from the transactional one. This document defines the problem and the verifiable outcomes; the solution design follows as a separate technical specification.
SECTION 02 · THE CORE PROBLEM
CONTENTION · TWO WORKLOADS, ONE DATABASE

Two jobs that don't share well sharing one machine.

The storefront needs thousands of tiny, instant writes. Analytics needs to scan months of history in one sweep. Run them on the same database and each degrades the other — exactly when it matters most, at peak.

WORKLOAD A · THE STOREFRONT

Transactions

Up to 75,000 orders/day at peak — high-frequency writes, sub-second reads. Every checkout, payment, and inventory update competes for the same locks.

WORKLOAD B · ANALYTICS

Reporting & insight

Large historical scans across the full order and payment history. Each report is a heavy, long-running read over the same tables the store is writing to.

WHEN THEY COLLIDE

The store slows

Analytical scans hold resources during peak — order processing waits behind reporting, putting revenue at risk in the busiest hours.

WHEN THEY COLLIDE

The reports crawl

Relentless write load starves analytics — a query that should take seconds takes minutes, so the business decides on yesterday's numbers.

THE ONE-LINE PROBLEM
A transactional system and an analytical system have fundamentally different needs — and forcing them onto one database means neither gets what it requires.

SECTION 07 · THE BOUNDARY
SCOPE · WHAT THIS INITIATIVE WILL AND WILL NOT DO

Solve the contention problem. Nothing more, this phase.

IN SCOPE · THIS PHASE
Separate the workloads
  • Isolate analytics from the transactional storefront
  • Interactive query performance on analytical data (R2)
  • Fresh, trustworthy data for reporting (R3)
  • Higher availability and faster incident recovery (R4, R5)
  • Reclaim data-engineering capacity from maintenance (R6)
vs
OUT OF SCOPE · FUTURE PHASE
Deferred, by design
  • Customer personalization & recommendation systems
  • Real-time machine-learning / predictive models
  • New customer-facing product features
  • Replatforming the transactional storefront itself
  • Net-new external data sources beyond today's system
WHY THE LINE SITS HERE
Personalization and real-time ML depend on a fast, reliable analytical foundation that does not exist yet. This initiative builds that foundation first — and is evaluated only on the contention outcomes above.

SECTION 09 · THE GROUND RULES
CONSTRAINTS & ASSUMPTIONS · WHAT WE'RE WORKING WITHIN

What the solution must respect — and what we take as given.

CONSTRAINT · NO DISRUPTION

The store keeps running

The transactional storefront cannot be slowed, paused, or risked. Any change must leave the order path untouched and be safe to roll back.

CONSTRAINT · SAME TEAM

Built by the team of 8

Delivered within the existing 8-engineer data team and current budget envelope — the goal is to free capacity, not to grow headcount.

ASSUMPTION · SOURCE OF TRUTH

The store stays authoritative

The transactional system remains the system of record for orders, customers, and payments. Analytics reads from it; it does not replace it.

ASSUMPTION · STABLE DEMAND

Today's volumes hold

Planning baselines: ~50K orders/day, 75K at peak, $85 AOV, 28% margin. Material changes to these inputs would reopen the targets above.

THE FRAME
This is a problem statement, not a design. It names the outcomes and the boundaries — the technical "how" is delivered separately as a specification that answers this document.
